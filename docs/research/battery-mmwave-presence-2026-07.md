# Battery-powered mmWave presence sensors (2026)

> **Revised 2026-07-23 (chg-2026-07-23-003):** the living room turns out to be physically hard to cable — "just power the FP2" is no longer the easy answer. This revision drops that framing, adds a full lux/temp/humidity capability + reporting-cadence pass (the question that actually decides "one battery device" vs. "battery presence + a spare Fibaro for environmentals"), and rewrites the Recommendation section accordingly. Original comparison table and product write-ups below are otherwise unchanged and still hold.

## Question
Does a battery-powered mmWave/radar presence sensor exist as a real shipping product in 2026 (Z-Wave preferred, Zigbee/Thread/Matter acceptable, Wi-Fi last resort) that's good enough to instrument a living room that cannot be cabled — ideally one that also reports usable lux/temp/humidity, so it replaces rather than duplicates a spare Fibaro multisensor?

## TL;DR
- **Yes, it's real, not vapourware** — but the category is under 18 months old. Every shipping SKU uses a **PIR-wake-then-radar** or **duty-cycled radar** trick; nobody runs 60 GHz continuously on a coin cell.
- **Z-Wave: none found battery-powered.** One certified Z-Wave mmWave sensor exists (Vision ZP3501ANZ-8) — it's mains/USB only. No chipmaker has shipped a Z-Wave-radio + duty-cycled-radar combo yet.
- **Aqara FP300 is the only one that's genuinely a single-device replacement for "battery presence + Fibaro multisensor."** Its Z2M exposes confirm real presence + PIR motion + lux (`lx`) + temperature (°C) + humidity (%), and — uniquely in this survey — its lux reporting interval is user-configurable down to 20 seconds (at a stated battery-life cost). Every other battery candidate either lacks temp/humidity entirely, reports a fake coarse lux "level" instead of real lux, or has no documented/controllable lux cadence at all.
- **Avoid sub-€20 Tuya clones** (ZG-204ZM) for anything you care about — a documented hardware-variant lottery causes permanent "stuck presence" on some units (traced to PCB flux residue), and its lux interval isn't even user-configurable in Z2M.
- **Revised recommendation given the no-cable constraint: Aqara FP300 alone**, Zigbee mode via Z2M, tuned conservatively (see Recommendation section) — not FP300 + a Fibaro, and not the FP2.

Two paragraphs on the two most relevant to this decision:

**Aqara FP300** is the most mainstream and best-documented battery mmWave sensor right now. It genuinely does PIR-triggered radar confirmation (not a relabeled PIR), Z2M support is comprehensive, and SmartHomeScene's teardown found the hybrid approach reliable after calibration. The catch, confirmed directly from the HA community forum thread for this device: it's a Zigbee "sleepy" end device, so **changing detection-distance/sensitivity settings requires physically pressing the button on the unit within ~10s of pushing the change from Z2M**, or the write silently doesn't take. Stock has also been genuinely spotty — a February 2026 HA-forum poster comparing FP300 vs Meross MS605 vs SwitchBot noted the FP300 was "never in stock in Canada."

**Tuya ZG-204ZM** is the cautionary tale for the "cheap and cheerful" end of this category. It is a real PIR+24GHz-radar hybrid, not a mislabeled PIR, and Z2M support is full. But GitHub issues (`Koenkk/zigbee2mqtt`, `ioBroker/ioBroker.zigbee`) document that specific hardware variants (`_TZE200_kb5noeto`) get permanently stuck reporting "presence detected," traced by the community to residual PCB solder flux — a manufacturing QC defect, not a config problem — with resoldering suggested as the only fix. Real-world battery reports in the same review are contradictory enough (2-3 weeks vs. "100% until the last day") to suggest the battery gauge itself is unreliable on this SKU, separate from the presence-stuck bug.

## FP300 "5-in-1" claim — verified, not just marketing copy

Confirmed against the Z2M device page (Aqara `PS-S04D`, kiosk-verify screenshot, 2026-07-23), not the marketing page. The full exposes list is: `power_outage_count, motion_sensitivity, battery, voltage, presence, pir_detection, presence_detection_options, ai_interference_source_selfidentification, ai_sensitivity_adaptive, absence_delay_timer, pir_detection_interval, illuminance, humidity, temperature, spatial_learning, restart_device, identify, temp_and_humidity_sampling, temp_and_humidity_sampling_period, temp_reporting_interval, temp_reporting_threshold, temp_reporting_mode, humidity_reporting_interval, humidity_reporting_threshold, humidity_report_mode, light_sampling, light_sampling_period, light_reporting_interval, light_reporting_threshold, light_report_mode, track_target_distance, target_distance, detection_range, detection_range_composite, led_disabled_night, schedule_start_time, schedule_end_time`.

That confirms the marketed "5-in-1" (presence, PIR motion, light, temperature, humidity) as genuinely real and genuinely exposed — Aqara isn't padding the count with battery/voltage/AI features the way some vendors do. If anything the marketing *undercounts*: there are 30+ real exposes once you include distance-tracking, AI interference rejection, and scheduling.

One more primary-source finding directly relevant to stationary-detection reliability (from the `pir_detection` expose description): **"in mmWave + PIR mode, after mmWave presence detection the PIR sensor gets turned off, so [pir_detection] might read false although presence is still detected."** In plain terms: Aqara's own documented architecture is PIR-triggers-first, then **radar alone sustains the occupied state** once confirmed — PIR isn't polled again while someone sits still. That directly answers the "does PIR-wake degrade stationary detection" question in the Recommendation section below.

## Patterns that recur

**Universal (= the physics answer to question 1):** every battery-mmWave product avoids continuous radar. It's either **PIR-wakes-radar** (Aqara FP300, SwitchBot, Tuya ZG-204ZM — PIR does the cheap always-on watching, radar only spins up to confirm stationary occupancy) or **aggressively duty-cycled pure radar** (ThirdReality R2, Zemismart ZPS-Z1 — radar itself pulses instead of running continuously, at the cost of a slower/less reliable "is someone still there" answer). Nobody ships continuous 60GHz on a coin cell. That's the actual innovation, not the radar chip itself.

**Only some share (= real choice axes):**
- **Chemistry**: CR2450 coin cell (Aqara, Zemismart, Meross) vs. AA/AAA (SwitchBot, ThirdReality, Tuya). AA/AAA capacity is larger but the devices using it lean more on PIR-gating to compensate for a hungrier radar; coin-cell devices seem to duty-cycle harder.
- **Protocol**: Zigbee dominates (native Z2M support in every case checked); Thread/Matter is newer and its config surface is thinner (FP300's own Matter-over-Thread mode loses zone/sensitivity config vs. its Zigbee mode); Bluetooth (SwitchBot) trades battery efficiency for a hub dependency that a real HA user flagged as a dealbreaker; **Z-Wave has nothing** in this category.
- **PIR-hybrid vs. pure-radar**: pure-radar (no PIR) needs a warm-up/calibration step (Zemismart: 5-10 min) that PIR-hybrids skip, because PIR gives them an instant "something moved" trigger for free.
- **Maturity tax**: every product here shipped or matured in 2024-2026. That shows up as either a config quirk (FP300's button-wake), a QC lottery (Tuya's flux defect), a stock problem (FP300, Meross), or simply "not enough real-world reports exist yet" (ThirdReality R2, Meross MS605).

## Recommendation (revised — no-cable constraint)

**Single device: Aqara FP300 in Zigbee mode via Z2M. Do not pair it with a Fibaro.** With the FP2 off the table (no cable run), FP300 is the only battery candidate that actually satisfies the "one device, one battery, one thing to fail" goal — it is the sole option in this survey with verified real lux + temperature + humidity in addition to presence, confirmed against its actual Z2M exposes rather than marketing copy. Adding a Fibaro back in would reproduce the two-battery, two-failure-point setup the user is explicitly trying to avoid, for environmental data FP300 already provides.

**Does the PIR-wake architecture hurt stationary detection? No, not for the case that matters — with one caveat on entry.** Aqara's own `pir_detection` expose description states the PIR sensor is turned off once mmWave confirms presence, and radar alone sustains the occupied state from then on. That means "someone sitting still and reading" is exactly the case this architecture handles correctly: PIR gets you the initial trigger, radar (not PIR) is what keeps the room "occupied" while they don't move again. The actual risk is at the *front door* of detection, not during it: if `presence_detection_options` is left on `both` (PIR-gated) and someone enters slowly enough to stay under the PIR motion threshold, the mmWave stage may never activate. Two ways to close that gap, in order of preference:
1. Set `presence_detection_options` to `mmwave` (radar-only, skip the PIR gate entirely) and accept whatever extra battery cost that carries — untested by anyone publicly, watch it for a month.
2. Leave it on `both` but keep `pir_detection_interval` low (near the 2s floor, not the 300s ceiling) — "higher detection frequency results in higher accuracy but lower battery life," per Z2M's own text — trading some battery for a lower miss chance on slow entries.

**Lux configuration: don't default to the fastest setting.** FP300 is the only device here where lux cadence is a dial you control, which is exactly why it's the right pick — but nobody has published what a 20-second `light_reporting_interval` costs the 3-year battery claim. Start on the `medium` preset (exact seconds undocumented but presumably well above FP2's already-adequate-for-most-automations ~2.5–5min), and only drop to `custom` with an interval in the 60–120s range if adaptive-lighting automations feel laggy in practice. Reserve the 20s floor for troubleshooting only, and watch battery drain for a month before committing to it long-term — this is a real, stated tradeoff, not a free upgrade over the FP2.

**Everything else in the survey is a worse fit for this specific goal, for a specific documented reason:**
- SwitchBot: real lux isn't available at all — it's a 5-level brightness bucket, useless for anything finer than "is it dark," plus a BLE-hub dependency an HA-forum user already rejected for range reasons.
- Tuya ZG-204ZM: has lux, but no configurable cadence in Z2M and a live hardware-defect lottery (stuck presence). Would still need a companion device, and might not even be a reliable presence sensor.
- ThirdReality R2: no light/temp/humidity at all — would need a Fibaro regardless, defeating the point.
- Meross MS605: has lux but no temp/humidity — still needs a companion device for those, plus the "instantly reports" claim is unverified and a sibling model has a known stale-sensor bug.
- Zemismart ZPS-Z1: has lux but zero published cadence information and needs a 5–10min calibration warm-up after every pairing.

Net: the no-cable constraint doesn't just make battery mmWave the primary candidate — it makes **FP300 specifically** the only candidate that closes the original "presence + Fibaro" pairing question cleanly. If FP300 stock is unavailable at the time of purchase (a real, documented risk — see the HA-forum thread below), the fallback is not another battery sensor from this table; it's re-checking whether *any* cable path exists (even a thin one, e.g. through a nearby skirting board or under carpet) before settling for a device with a documented capability gap.
